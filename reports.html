Final In-Depth Report of AIFLD's Activities in Peru: Covering the Period from the Inception of the Program in 1963 to its Termination on May 16, 1971
American Institute for Free Labor Development
Date: 1971
As the authors stated in the introductory remarks, "In this report we have tried to present an accurate picture of AIFLD's accomplishments over the comprised nine-year period. We feel that considering the difficulties involved, many of them outside our control, the accomplishments of the program were considerable. The conditions of the Peruvian world of his organization were better for our efforts. This is not true of the existence of many serious problems both external and internal to the democratic labor movement. It is to argue strongly that at the time it would be most unwise to leave the labor field wide open to extremist and other forces which have every intention of destroying the free and democratic labor movement, as we know it. Obvious patterns and programs are needed."

Landlessness in Nicaragua
Oulahan, Richard
Date: February 21, 1992
In this report, Oulahan describes the issue of landlessness in Nicaragua, which refers to the fact that thousands of families in Nicaragua do not have land. While there are vacant lands available, their owners do not allow anyone who doesn't own them to occupy or cultivate them. This has resulted in significant losses to the economy, and the government has not yet taken measures to address this issue.
